Summary: the design's central point is information-carrying image set and user is guided trough it.

  • Scrolling is better than clicking. Instead of gallery and zoom widgets it shows user all the details during scrolling down.
  • The layout is simple. If you want a responsive design, complex layout will limit your work on usability.
  • It's easy to differentiate between product info and "add to cart"/"buy" areas at a glance.
  • Instead of "manual zoom in" a user is guided through important details by sequence of "photo + description" blocks. You can't be sure she's aware of all benefits and functionality she should pay attention to.
  • Photos are big enough to show interesting details to users who scroll quickly and don't pay full attention.
  • It's a boilerplate. Just a starting point. It's minimalist/unfinished on purpose. It should be edited.
